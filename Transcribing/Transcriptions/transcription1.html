Transcription 1: The Book of Deer (CUL I.i.6.32)

A foundation legend of the monastery of Deer (mod. Aberdeenshire) and a set of property records, in Middle Gaelic, added to the Book of Deer, a late first millenium Latin Gospel Book produced under Irish influence somewhere in the north of Britain. The Gaelic texts were almost certainly inscribed at Deer. They are in Middle Gaelic but with features distinctive to Scottish Gaelic. In addition, there are two short specimens of Old Gaelic within the Gospel Book proper of less certain provenance.
