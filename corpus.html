Manuscript Details: Edinburgh NLS Adv. MS. 72.2.2

NLS Adv. MS 72.2.2 is mostly a 17th-/18th-century collection of bardic poetry both inscribed and, in many cases, composed by members of the MacMhuirich family. It also includes several leaves from NLS Adv. MS 72.1.34, by Eoghan Mac Phail, which also contain poetry.

Four poems are included in this transcription, 1 and 2 inscribed by Eoghan mac Phail, 3 by Domhnall Mac Mhuirich, and 4 by Niall Mac Mhuirich: (1) 'Goll mear mileat', a panegyric of Goll mac Morna, Finn mac Cumaill's legendary opponent; (2) 'An bfhaca tusa thu fein', a love poem; (3) 'Maith an sgel do sgaoil nar miosg', a praise poem for Ailin, Chief of Clanranald c. 1696; (4) 'Domhnall mac raghnuill rosg mall', an anonymous but potentially near-contemporary panegyric of the eponymous ancestor of Clann Domhnaill. 3 and 4 have been chosen deliberately as examples of late and early bardic poetry respectively.

Notes

1. The edge of the page runs right along the top of the letters in line 1, meaning some accents or abbreviation markers may have been lost. The most obviously required accents have been supplied. Only visible abbreviations are indicated.

2. This adjective is evidently related to fíad ("game") or fíad ("wild land") but the only available lemma for "fiadhach" means "hunting", which would not fit here syntactically unless one imagine a genitive plural along the clunky lines of "of the huntings" or suchlike.

3. This could be taken as the comparative of solus ("brighter the face of Ó Colla") or the noun, soillse ("sunshine"), in an implied copula construction ("The face of Ó Colla [is] sunshine"). Ther problem with the former is that "dech", in line b, already supplies a comparative.

4. This line is one syllable short; "nglan" is found before "ngorm" in other manuscripts.
