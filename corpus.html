Manuscript Details: Edinburgh NLS Adv. 72.1.2

NLS Adv. 72.1.2 is a large and diverse manuscript associated with the Beaton family. While it contains a large number of medical texts, other fields of learning, such as logic and cosmology, are also well-represented. Furthermore, some of the medical items are unusual, compared to what is available from other manuscripts. A selection of of texts from the manuscript is presented here, with opportunities to transcribe material on otherwise poorly represented subjects being given priority.

Notes

1. There is a suspension stroke following the "-h" of "rath-" but I have been unable to find a cogent expansion for this.

2. Again, there is a suspension stroke following the "-h" of "rath-" but I have been unable to find a cogent expansion for this.

3. This should be expressing the idea that the individual is the lowest and most specific division, but the expansions are uncertain.

4. The text here repeats itself almost twice over. One repetition could be due to eye-skip but two is less explicable. It is also not clear where the repetition begins ("agus ata[it]" or " quid .ii. dibh"). Closer analysis of the argument is required to establish what is most likely. It is interesting to note, if this is a simple case of eye-skip, that the spelling and forms are slightly different in each repetion, even though the context is the same. This may yield insights into the scribe's methodology and faithfulness at the granular level to their source.
